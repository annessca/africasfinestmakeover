How Faith and attitude can enhance beauty



Your reality of being

If you have ever sat in a room where an individual underwent some form of a make-over, you would have observed that the attitude such a person carried before the make-over gradually changed into an entirely different one. And by the time finishing touches were being delicately brushed up here and there, your transformed human must have been bursting with pride and confidence. What brings about the drastic behavioral change? It is simply because the person has experienced an undeniable awareness of a new reality of being. The suddenly transformed person knows that he or she is in possession of the power of beauty and also understands the intimidating effect his or her newly acquired look is having on the people who care to watch.

The consciousness you have of your reality of being either makes you stand tall or stoop and fold. When you realize that you have all the right accessories on you, that you're wearing the best dress that can be found in the high-end stores of Millan, that people can't seem to take their eyes of your 5 inches Prada, and that your make-up is flawless enough to feature on Vogue magazine, then you will mount your high horse like you deserve it.


Your awareness of self

It is very typical for women to keep their early rising looks away from the people who are of any major significance to them. Such people are not allowed to see her face that's not been touched even with white talcum. That face won't make a great impression for her. So women feel very uncomfortable and miserable, whenever they have that dreadful accidental encounter. When a woman is of the awareness that she is not looking good enough, she tends to coil up, losing all her confidence. But observe the same woman with a certain awareness of good looks and a superior style, then you will see an overtly confident woman with all the right attitude to capture as much attention as she can. That is how powerful the conscious awareness of self can be.


Faith can do much good

When you believe something to be true, you will act like that is your reality. When you don't have faith in something, your inability to perceive any truth in it will also influence your reality. Your faith or lack of it will fuel your attitude towards what you believe to be true. If you start acting all confident and even arrogant, it is because there is something you know to be true. Such will not be the case if you have nothing to fuel your ego. You feel validated by what you believe to be true. So in any case, if you can believe anything you chose to believe, then your faith forms the basis and entirety of your reality.

When you believe that you don't look good enough, you will feel deflated, you will appear defeated and everyone who sees you will definitely agree with you. But if on the other hand, you can consciously and continuously make a reaffirmation to your inner dialog that you are beautiful, then having or not having any artificial beauty enhancements, your attitude becomes positive and your confidence skyrockets. People will naturally respond to you in resonance to the manner they perceive you. You have the power of faith to mold a favorable attitude for yourself. If you look down on yourself, it will be obvious and people will help you accomplish that very well. But if you would rather believe you are the most beautiful person that's ever been created by God, it will also be obvious and that is the response you will get from the rest of the world.
